FAQ'S

We manufacture components from a variety of materials, including polyamide, polyacetal, plexiglass, fluoroplastic, polypropylene, polyethylene, PEEK, rubber, silicone, silicone rubber, rubber, polyurethane, circuit boards and fiberglass. We manufacture these parts based on provided drawings, sketches or physical samples.

Plastics manufacturing processes include injection molding, extrusion, blow molding, and thermoforming, among others. Each process has its own strengths and is suitable for different applications. For example, injection molding is ideal for producing complex parts in high volumes, while thermoforming is more cost-effective for large, simple shapes. The choice of manufacturing process must match the part design and production volume.

For plastic parts, several design factors are critical, including wall thickness, draft angles, undercuts, and material selection. Designing for manufacturability can help reduce costs and improve the quality of the final product. Proper wall thickness, draft angles for easy removal, and minimizing undercuts are just a few examples that can make your design more cost-effective and efficient to manufacture.

Quality control in custom plastics manufacturing typically involves inspection, testing and process monitoring. Manufacturers often use methods such as visual inspection, dimensional inspection, and material testing to ensure that products meet specified requirements. ISO and other quality management standards can also be followed. It is important to discuss quality control processes with your chosen manufacturer to ensure they meet the quality expectations for your project.
